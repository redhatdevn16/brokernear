What is FxPro?

FxPro is a successful multinational online broker headquartered in the United Kingdom. The brand provides financial services like forex and Contracts for Difference (CFDs) to clients worldwide on different asset classes. Since its launch in 2006, the company has maintained some of the best user-friendly trading platforms, competitive prices, and excellent customer service support.

Their operations are currently licensed by several reputable financial authorities, including the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) in the UK, the Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC), the South Africa Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA), and the Securities Commission of the Bahamas (SCB).

The company has received about 84 commendations in the last decade from renowned trading communities and stakeholders. A few of these awards include "best trading platform," "best FX provider," and "best forex trading platform." On average, The broker firm receives about five awards annually, a remarkable achievement that few internet brokerage firms can claim.

According to these regulators, and based on trust score ratings of 90 out of 99, It is a low-risk broker company. Its activities show it is committed to providing transparent trading practices. An excellent way to confirm that the firm isn't a scam is by studying how it handles and protects clients' funds differently.

Funds Segregation

The brokerage company separates all customer's funds/assets from company funds. All client assets are kept in various bank accounts in Europe. The firm does this to assure customers that their funds are not used to make further investments, cover running costs, or any other company expenses.

Investor compensation fund (ICF)

Brokering companies affiliated with Cyprus Investment Firms (CIFs) mandatorily participate in the ICF. It is one of the country's regulatory requirements. It is a reputable member of the ICF. It saves some clients' funds here and ensures all covered clients receive their claims promptly.

Negative balance protection

The company offers clients negative balance protection, which saves them from losing more than they deposited. Simply put, you cannot owe the company more than you have on the account.

For more context, consider the following scenario. If you deposit $2,000 into your broker account and engage in a CFD trade using 5:1 leverage, your position is worth $10,000. However, if the position's value drops by 25%, your loss would be $2,500, which is 25% of $10,000.

This means that your current balance of $2,000 would not cover the losses, resulting in a debt owed to the broker of $500. However, if the same trade is conducted on FXpro, which offers negative balance protection, the losses will be, at most, the initial deposit of $2,000. Therefore, instead of having a negative balance of $500, the account balance will be $0.

These fund protection strategies make it one of the top choices any investor, regardless of their skill level, could make when opening a spot or live trading account.

Europe 50 CFD: Low fees and an average of 1.25 points during peak hours.

EURUSD: Low fees and an average of 1.71 points during peak hours.

These trading fees make it easy for clients to trade at various times weekly. Note that trading costs may be significantly higher when trading on margins or holding long positions. There are other fees you have to pay on the website. They include the following:

Currency conversion fees

The firm charges a currency conversion fee on all trade pairs different from the currencies in your wallets. The amount is determined by the exchange rate obtained from liquidity providers and the markup.

Non-trading fees

It does not charge most non-trading-related activity done on your account.

Here are some of the non-trading activities.

  • Deposit fee
  • Withdrawal fee
  • Inactivity fee

Deposit fee: This applies when you transfer funds from your regular bank account to your trading account. The platform issues no charges for deposits. Therefore, what you transfer out of your bank account is the same amount that enters your brokerage account, regardless of the deposit method.

Withdrawal fees: The platform does not charge a withdrawal fee for transferring money from your brokerage account to your bank account. You can complete these transactions via electronic wallets, bank transfers, and credit/debit cards.

Inactivity fee: This is the only non-trading activity charged on the website. The company charges a $15 account maintenance fee and a $5 monthly fee after six months of inactivity. This brokering website is not suitable if you're a buy-and-hold investor, except if you're willing to be strategic about your trading activity.

Spread

The firm offers two types of bids and asks spreads in forex trading. They are variable and fixed spreads. Variable spreads change with market conditions, and fixed spreads remain constant. It offers competitive spreads through its platform, which aggregates price quotes from over 12 liquidity providers and offers to trade them against tier-1 liquidity.

It offers variable and fixed spreads in its MT5 accounts with zero commission. The MT4 account has fixed spreads on seven major forex pairs, while the cTrader account offers tight spreads with a commission charge. The variable spread accounts use market execution and do not have commission costs, leading to wider spreads than ECN-priced and commissioned accounts from other brokers.

The leading broker company offers these varieties of mobile trading options to give traders the flexibility and convenience they need to manage their trading accounts and stay updated on market news.
