eToro review 2023

The company was founded in 2007 with the goal of making financial markets accessible to everyone. It serves over 25 million traders and investors based in about 140 nations.

eToro review 2023 looks to provide an in-depth study of the eToro trading platform and covers its key features and somewhat "pricey" services. If you're a novice, experienced, or social trader in the financial industry, this will help you make an informed decision about whether eToro is the right broker for your needs.

What is eToro?

It is an innovative platform that allows users to study and copy the trades of other investors on its social investing platform. In addition, the company runs multi-asset brokerages and offers eToro stocks, ETFs, commodities, forex, and cryptocurrencies.

eToro trading platform seeks to encourage more people to trade and invest in diverse markets and assets. The company is headquartered in Tel Aviv, Israel, but has other UK, US, and Cyprus offices.

The company is active on its website and mobile app, which have a help center for all customers, including a customer service section and a complaint process. Popular eToro reviews laud the platform's exclusive perks for sharing trading strategies and its 3,000 tradable assets.

Below are the services offered on the eToro trading platform:

Manual investing

Through its innovative investment platform, it provides a diverse range of assets, including eToro stocks, currencies, commodities, crypto, ETFs, and indices. In addition, clients can use professional tools and analyses to track and invest in various financial instruments.

Social Investing

It allows clients to follow the financial activities of other investors and use the CopyTrader system to replicate their trades in real time. In addition, the eToro platform is transparent, as it shows every client's data, like risk score, gain percentage, and portfolio composition.

The Investor program

It enables successful investors to grow their businesses by sharing their expertise and getting paid by eToro for being copied. This program is built on the CopyTrader feature and is unique to the platform.

Smart Portfolios

It also offers a ready-made investment option consisting of top investors from the platform or a combination of assets following a predetermined market strategy.

eToro Money app

It offers a cash account and debit card that users can use for bank transfers, deposits, withdrawals, and shopping anywhere in the world. Currently, the cash account and debit card are available exclusively to eToro traders in the UK.

The eToro Money app includes the eToro Money Crypto Wallet, a user-friendly, multi-crypto, secure digital wallet for sending and receiving crypto assets.

eToro Pros and Cons

Pros

User-friendly trading experience: The platform is designed to be user-friendly and accessible, with a clear and intuitive interface. This makes it easy for traders of all experience levels to navigate and make trades.

CopyTrader feature: The brokerage is renowned for its social trading feature, CopyTrader, which allows traders to connect and copy the trades of successful traders. This can be especially useful for new traders who are still learning the ropes.

This unusual feature may also appeal to seasoned cryptocurrency traders who have the potential to earn annual rewards of up to 2% of their average balance by expanding their following on the platform.

Cons

Location-dependent services: Residents of New York, Nevada, Minnesota, and Hawaii cannot trade stocks and cryptocurrencies with it as the platform does not support these activities in these states. However, they can still sign up for a free account and participate in virtual trading.

Customer service: The firm's customer service could be better. The staff are primarily rude, impatient, or fail to reply or give relevant responses.

Leverage

It offers its users leverage, allowing them to trade with more money than they have in their accounts. This can magnify potential gains but also increases the risk of losses.

The amount of leverage offered depends on several factors, such as the traders' jurisdiction regulations and the asset being traded. In some regions, regulations limit the amount of leverage offered. For example, the European Union's maximum leverage is limited to 30:1 for major currency pairs. You should also recall that you can work with eToro forex and stocks.

Overall, eToro's offering aligns with industry standards and allows traders to trade with greater exposure to the markets while being mindful of the associated risks.

Trading Fees

Compared to traditional broker firms, trading on eToro costs considerably lower trading fees. eToro charges clients commissions, spreads, and financial rates for various types of transactions. Here are more details on the trading fees:

Conversion fees

All eToro traders, except US-based users, must convert their currency into the platform's available base currency, USD.

If you are based in a non-US nation and wish to deposit or withdraw funds from an account, you will need to exchange your USD. The conversion charge for major currencies like GBP, EUR, CHF, or AUD is 50–150 pip or approximately 0.4% of the total sum, depending on the current exchange rate.

If you decide to deposit or withdraw funds through debit/credit cards or PayPal, the conversion fee begins from 150 pips.

Other fees

Withdrawal fees: At eToro, the withdrawal cost is a set of $5 for every transaction. The smallest withdrawal allowed is $30.

Commission fees: There are no commissions, ticket, rollover, management, or additional broker’s fees after trading on eToro.

Spread

eToro, as a market maker, sets its own spreads, which are average compared to other market makers. For example, the spread on the most liquid currency pair, EUR/USD, at eToro is quoted at 1.0 pips.

However, spreads on other assets, such as equity and index CFDs, commodities, and cryptocurrencies, tend to be higher compared to other brokers.

Compared to other brokers, eToro has high and less affordable spreads, generally making it difficult for beginners to profit. For example, the spread on AMD is $0.15, while competitors offer the same CFD for $0.03. In addition, gold on the platform has a spread as high as 45.0 pips compared to 1.0 pips offered by other brokers, and the spread on the S&P 500 is 75.0 pips versus 0.60 pips.

The spread of cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin is also displayed as a percentage, with Bitcoin at 0.75% at eToro versus 5.00 pips at other brokers.

Mobile Trading Platform

The mobile trading app is the same as the web version but with limited customization options. Additionally, it feels more like a social media app than a trading app.

Usability

The navigation is straightforward and user-friendly, partly because there is little content to work with. Some icons could be clearer and labeled, but there is not much of a learning curve. In addition, the consistency between platforms enhances their ease of use.

Watchlists

These are simple, organized, and minimal compared to traditional brokers. The display includes the name, symbol, a small chart, price, and change. Customization options are limited. Clicking on an item leads to more information, including posts, stats, a chart, research, and news.

Chart views

There are three chart views available. The first is on the overview page, which is mainly helpful for quickly monitoring the asset's performance. The charts on the stats page offer more time frames, and the dedicated chart page allows you to choose chart types and add indicators, from which there are five options.

Deposits and Withdrawals

You can access funds in your eToro account by withdrawing up to the account balance minus any margin used. You don't have to keep funds in your account, so you can withdraw the total balance if desired. The system will send the withdrawn funds to the relevant account that made the deposit.

For example, if you deposited with a credit card, the system will send the funds back to that credit card. Withdrawal methods have priority, with the credit card first followed by PayPal, then bank transfers.

If your information has changed, eToro may request an alternate withdrawal method. All withdrawals from eToro are subject to a $5 flat fee. The smallest withdrawal is $30.

You will also be charged a conversion fee if you withdraw money in a currency other than USD. The conversion charge will change based on the currency you choose. In addition, once you complete the transaction, your bank, credit card company, or PayPal may charge you a transfer fee.

Conclusion

eToro is a platform shaking up the traditional trading industry with its innovative approach. With a user-friendly design, social media-inspired touch, and a wide range of assets, it is fast making trading accessible to a new generation of investors.

While its offerings, including leverage and watch lists, may not be as extensive as those of established brokers, it provides a simple and intuitive platform for new and experienced traders alike. Overall, eToro is a platform that deserves consideration for those looking for a unique and innovative approach to trading.
