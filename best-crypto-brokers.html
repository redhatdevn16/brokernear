What is a crypto broker?

Crypto brokers are online platforms where investors can profitably trade or exchange different tokens. These firms make digital asset trades easy by directly linking you with the cryptocurrency market.

Cryptocurrency brokerage firms offer various services, instruments, as well as accounts. The local financial authorities in a firm's operational locations determine the policies regulating their brokerage services.

Some brokers allow you to trade coins at their prices, some help you fill orders, while others trade other cryptocurrencies derivatives like Contracts for Difference (CFDs). But, to access any of this, you must complete a registration process.

Registering with a cryptocurrency brokerage firm is straightforward. All you need to start is an active email address, proof of identity, and a password.

Regulated brokers verify all customer identities by requesting full names, contact addresses, current utility bills, and photo IDs. This considerably limits the occurrence of fraud on the websites.

Although the best crypto brokers offer free discounted services, they successfully generate revenues from fees like spreads and commissions and non-trading fees like withdrawal, deposit, inactivity and overnight charges.

Popular Crypto Trading Strategies

Here are some strategies you can implement when making digital asset trades:

Contracts for difference (CFDs)

CFDs are derivative assets that allow you to profit from the price of virtual currencies without owning them. When you invest in CFDs, you are bound by a contract to pay the difference between the asset's current price and its price at the termination of the contract.

CFDs are easy to trade and have a fast execution time. It is easy to leverage these assets to increase your profits. Note that they are high-risk instruments and primarily unsuitable for new traders.

Long and short positions

Long and short positions are common trade strategies for brokers who can predict the value of an asset.

Taking a long position means purchasing the asset. You can do this when you predict the price of an asset will rise, and when it finally does, you get to sell at a profit.

Taking a short position means indirectly selling tokens that you predict will fall in price. If the price falls as expected, you profit. But, if it rises, you make a loss. Traders often use margin accounts to borrow coins from brokers for this.

Tools like signals, trading bots, news updates, and technical indicators help traders make credible predictions.

Futures contracts

Futures are complex assets that offer traders an opportunity to bet on the future worth of a decentralized token. It is a contract to trade a fixed amount of tokens at predetermined prices on a future date.

Traders use futures to hedge funds. You can use leverage for futures contracts. However, understand that it could amplify your losses like it makes your profits. Try paper trading futures before you attempt real-time trades.

Frequently Asked Questions
