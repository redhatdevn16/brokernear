The Best Forex Trading Strategies That Work

Forex trading is a financial jungle that many traders try to navigate in the hopes of striking gold. It involves buying and selling different currencies with the aim of making a profit. However, Forex trading is not for the faint-hearted, and traders need to have a good understanding of the market and the various strategies that they can use to make profitable trades. In this article, we will take a closer look at some of the best Forex trading strategies that work.

Trend Following

One popular strategy is trend following, which involves identifying the direction of the market and trading in the same direction. The aim is to ride the wave when the market is trending upward and jump ship when it is trending downwards. This strategy works well in a market that is trending strongly, but it may not work well in a market that is consolidating.

Legendary hedge fund managers and professional traders often use the 200-day moving average as a key tool to identify the direction of a trend. This old trend filter has been adopted by some of the greatest traders of all time, such as billionaire trader Paul Tudor Jones. According to Jones, the 200-day moving average of closing prices is his metric for everything he looks at. He believes that using this rule can help traders avoid losing everything and play defense by getting out of the market when necessary.

In practical terms, if the price is above the 200-day exponential moving average (EMA), we can assume that we are in an uptrend. On the other hand, if the price is below the 200-day EMA, we can assume that we are in a downtrend. By using this simple yet effective trend filter tool, traders can potentially improve their method of identifying trends and make better investment decisions.

According to a study by Trade Captain, trend-following strategies have a success rate of around 30-40%.

Range Trading

Range trading is another popular Forex trading strategy that involves identifying a range in which the market is trading and buying at the bottom of the range and selling at the top of the range. This strategy works well in a market that is consolidating but may not work well in a market that is trending strongly.

Advantages of Trading Ranges:

Easily Identifiable Trading Levels - With a range on the chart, traders can easily identify the high and low points of a horizontal channel. This provides a clear indication of when a price bounce in the opposite direction is expected, making it easier to identify potential trading opportunities.

Early Entry into Potential Trends - Valid breakouts from a range can provide traders with the opportunity to get in early on a potential trend extension. Trading the initial breakout can lead to a favorable Reward to Risk ratio and potentially result in significant profits if the breakout extends into a sizable impulse leg.

DailyFX reports that range trading strategies have a success rate of around 55%.

Breakout Trading

Breakout trading is a Forex trading strategy that involves identifying a key level of support or resistance and trading in the direction of the breakout. The aim is to catch the wave when the price breaks above the resistance level and bail out when the price breaks below the support level. This strategy works well in a market that is trending strongly, but it may not work well in a market that is consolidating.

If you're looking for a way to enter trades, consider using the breakout entry strategy. One approach is to wait for the price to breach a resistance level before opening a position. For many traders, this indicates that the price has the momentum to go higher, as a breach of resistance may suggest bullish sentiment.

Of course, this isn't always the case, but some traders still find this breakout from a resistance level a useful entry point. Conversely, you can also use the breakout entry when the price has breached a support level. This breach of support is often seen as a signal that prices may drop further, and some traders take advantage of these falls in prices.

To use this entry strategy, you'll need to understand what support and resistance levels are and how to identify them. You can do this by using the charting function of the MT4 trading platform, whether you're using a demo or live account. Try identifying support and resistance levels on various instruments and time frames to gain confidence in using this strategy.

According to a study by DailyFX, breakout trading strategies have a success rate of around 30-40%.

Scalping

Scalping is a forex trading style that involves opening and closing multiple positions on one or more currency pairs within a day, often in mere seconds or minutes. Instead of holding one position throughout a trend, scalpers make several quick trades along the way.

Scalpers aim to profit from small price movements, earning just a few pips at a time. A pip is a standard unit of measurement in forex trading that represents the fourth decimal place of a currency pair's price. For instance, a drop from 1.3980 to 1.3979 would equal one pip.

However, some currency pairs like the Japanese yen are quoted to two decimal places.

To trade currency pairs that are rising or falling, scalpers often use derivatives such as CFDs. If they anticipate an uptrend, they'll buy (go long), and if they expect a downtrend, they'll sell (go short).

Leveraged products like CFDs allow traders to open positions with a margin deposit, which can increase potential profits but also magnify losses. Thus, having a sound risk management strategy is essential regardless of the scalping techniques used.

DailyFX reports that scalping strategies have a success rate of around 10-15%.

Carry Trading

Carry trading is a Forex trading strategy that involves taking advantage of the interest rate differential between two currencies. The aim is to buy the currency with a higher interest rate and sell the currency with a lower interest rate.

When engaging in an FX carry trade, traders borrow currency in a country with a low-interest rate and use it to purchase currency in a country with a high-interest rate. The currency with the lower interest rate is known as the "funding currency," while the currency with the higher interest rate is referred to as the "target currency." By holding the position overnight, traders earn interest based on the positive carry of the trade.

Interest rates are set by a country's central bank and vary from country to country. Traders earn interest when they are long the currency in the pair with the higher interest rate. Changes in interest rates and exchange rate appreciation/depreciation are the two main components of the FX carry trade. While traders profit from overnight interest payments, they can also lose money if the target currency depreciates against the funding currency, erasing positive interest payments. It's crucial to have a risk management strategy in place when participating in an FX carry trade.

Position Trading

Position trading is a trading style that involves focusing on longer-term price movements in the market. Rather than frequently trading, position traders hold a small number of major positions for several months to a year or longer. However, they may still actively trade around those positions. This strategy requires a deep understanding of fundamental factors that can influence prices over the long term and the use of technical analysis to determine the best timing for entering and exiting positions within the longer market cycle.

Successful position traders typically have expertise in macroeconomic data and use fundamental analysis to identify trading opportunities. They then use technical analysis to help time their trades for optimal entry and exit points.

Position trading strategies have a success rate of around 50-60%, according to DailyFX studies.

News Reversal Trading

The News Reversal Strategy involves trading the news after its release by focusing on a sudden, sustained reversal in price direction after an initial strong move in one direction. This reversal could be caused by algorithms or a collective feeling among the market that the initial price reaction was an overreaction. However, this strategy has a downside in that there may be no reversal and the price continues to move in the direction of the initial spike.

To implement this strategy, traders should look out for a spike in price immediately after the news release. They can then wait for 10-15 minutes for a potential reversal that could bring the price back to pre-release levels. Traders can enter a position as the price breaks above or below these levels and consider setting multiple target levels. Once one target is reached, traders can take profits on half of the position and adjust the stop on the remaining position to break even.

According to a study by DailyFX, news trading strategies have a success rate of around 25-30%.

Swing Trading

Swing trading refers to a Forex trading approach where trades are held for a few days or weeks to capture medium-term market movements while avoiding short-term market fluctuations. This strategy is effective for traders with expertise in technical analysis, enabling them to identify market trends.

DailyFX's research indicates that swing trading strategies tend to have a success rate of approximately 40-50%.

Price Action Trading

Price action trading is a Forex trading strategy that involves analyzing the price movements of a currency pair without the use of indicators. The aim is to identify key levels of support and resistance and to trade accordingly. This strategy works well for traders who have a good understanding of price action and are able to interpret market movements without the use of indicators.

To trade based on price action, it is important to analyze the trending waves and pullback waves, which are also known as impulse and corrective waves. A trend is considered to be progressing when the trending waves are larger than the corrective waves.

Traders can identify the direction of the trend by monitoring "swing highs" and "swing lows" as well as the length of the trending and pullback waves. In an uptrend, the price should make higher swing highs and higher swing lows, while in a downtrend, the opposite is true. The peaks and troughs of trendlines can be observed floating between lines of support and resistance on a price chart.

According to a study by DailyFX, price action trading strategies have a success rate of around 40-50%.

Wrapping Up

In conclusion, Forex trading is a financial jungle that requires a lot of skill, knowledge, and guts. The best Forex trading strategies are those that work for you and fit your trading style. By using a combination of these strategies and practicing good risk management, traders can increase their chances of making profitable trades in the Forex market. Remember, the Forex market can be a wild ride, so always be prepared for the unexpected and never put all your eggs in one basket.
